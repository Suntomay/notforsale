Francis of Assisi’s View of Animals

Francis of Assisi saw animals not just as living beings, but as fellow creatures of God, with a right to life, love, and respect. For him, all of creation was sacred, and he believed that humans had a special responsibility to protect animals and treat them justly. At a time when animals were often viewed as inferior or purely utilitarian, Francis taught compassion and respect for all creatures.

His deep love for animals and nature makes Francis a symbol of environmental protection and animal welfare to this day. October 4, his feast day, is also celebrated as World Animal Day to highlight the rights and protection of animals worldwide.

Francis of Assisi's life and his message of respect for nature and all living beings remain timeless and inspiring. They remind us to treat animals and the environment with care and to view them as valuable and equal parts of creation.

Looking for a home

Lena – don’t stop believin

A happy ending for Lena. Lena, a street dog, was admitted to Rebecca's Dog Shelter Chalchidiki in Greece very late in life. Because she had a damaged hip and was incontinent, she desperately needed a warm, homely environment. It's important to us to give dogs, especially older dogs, a good home with dignity and recognition.

And we're delighted to see how Lena blossoms more and more every day and enjoys her new life. Save the dogs!

Maxima – what’s new?

"You're never too old to try something new!" A ten-year-old German Shepherd mix was being treated for heartworms at an animal shelter in Lower Austria. We took her in and nursed her back to health. Now she's a very sociable, fun "helper" in our family, learning quickly and happily, even in her old age.

Phoebe – please be quiet!

"Control is my job!" Phoebe, an elderly, anxious dog, was rescued from an animal shelter in Lower Austria as soon as possible because she was struggling in the kennel. After her first owner died, she was brought to the shelter.

Since Christmas 2021, she has been living in a quiet environment and is cared for by the retired couple Jari and Paul. As a companion dog, she supports her owner in her daily life.
